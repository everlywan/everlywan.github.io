Personal Summary

I immigrated to the U.S. in 2022 and became a student at Cherry Creek High School in Colorado. I’m proud of the academic record I’ve built—earning A’s in each single class, maintaining a 4.375 weighted GPA and a 4.000 unweighted GPA—but grades alone don’t define me.

What defines me is the way I respond to unfamiliarity—with curiosity, and with quiet persistence. Whether it was adapting to a new language, navigating cultural differences, or asking my first question in an American classroom, I’ve learned to face discomfort not with fear, but with a kind of creative patience. I challenge myself constantly and never stop exploring new things. I joined FBLA for the first time this year—and walked away with first-place awards at both the school and district levels. More than the trophy, I’m proud of the confidence I built in the process. I still remember when public speaking made me tremble. Now, I speak with clarity and purpose, not because I was born confident, but because I earned it.

Outside of class, I write novels, draw digitally, and create educational videos on YouTube, teaching math, science, economics, and vocabulary—over 100 videos and counting. I believe learning doesn’t stop at the edge of a desk. It travels, it echoes, and it lifts others. That’s why I’ve also volunteered for the past three years at Denver Rescue Mission and served as a Chinese language assistant—because sharing knowledge teaches me patience, empathy, and human connection.

My aim isn’t just to be a great student. It’s to become someone who makes the world better—through education, creativity, and responsibility. I don’t try to be the loudest voice in the room. I try to be the one who listens deeply, leads quietly, and builds things that matter. I believe that wherever I go next, any college that truly sees me will see not just what I’ve done, but who I’ve become—and everything I still have to give.

That’s what makes me, me.
